Chapter 2

Life itself is but what you deem it. - Marcus Aurelius. Happiness starts with improving your own way of thinking, not by forcing the world around you to adapt to your wishes. If you choose to focus only on the bad things, your whole life will seem bad to you.

The progress principle: pleasure comes more from making progress toward goals than from achieving them. This one is also true for getting better outcomes in life. As James Clear said: if you want better results, then forget about setting goals. Focus on your system instead. Goals are good for setting a direction, but systems are best for making progress.

The adaptation principle: in the long run, it doesn’t much matter what happens to you. Good fortune or bad, you will always return to your happiness set point -your brain’s default level of happiness- which was determined largely by your genes.

Happiness doesn't come just from within, or even from a combination of internal and external factors, but from between.
